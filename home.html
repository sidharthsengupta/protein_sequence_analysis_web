This site contains some simple predictive tools that utilize amino acid sequence

Look through and click on the name of a protein above to get its sequence which you can use with the tools linked in the navigation bar at the top.If you have a protein in mind you can go to the Protein Data Bank or Uniprot to find its sequence. Please only copy text that contains the sequence in 1-letter code. You can use the included clean button to remove spaces and line breaks before analyzing the sequence.

These services are not intended for professional or academic use. As outlined in the methodologies, most algorithms are simplified emulations of professional tools. For questions and concerns, please contact sengupsi@oregonstate.edu.





Myc

Transcriptional regulator that is involved in a variety of cellular functions. Mutated forms have been implicated in carcinogenesis. One of the original factors that was used to restore pluripotency in somatic cells.

Tumor Supressor p53

Essential tumor supressor gene that repairs DNA damage. It has been described as the "guardian of the genome" for its function in preventing cancer-causing mutations.

Hypoxia-Inducible Factor 1-alpha

Transcriptional regulator during periods of hypoxia. Activates and suppresses genes in order to increase oxygen delivery and ensure cell survival.

Hemoglobin

Metalloprotein that complexes with iron in order to bind oxygen and transport it throughout the body.

Heat Shock Protein 70

DNA Polymerase

An essential member of the DNA replication process. Catalyzes the synthesis of new DNA from an existing strand. Some forms can also correct mistakes in replication.
